How The Floor Works

The rewards layer, in full.

The Floor is the rewards side of APESONUS. You listen, you back the artists you believe in, and when the season builds toward a peak, everyone holding a position shares in a reward pool that we and our partners put up. Here is exactly how it works, numbers included, so nothing about it is a mystery.

Listening and Ammo

The music plays on Ammo. Every day you get five free plays, on any track. After your five, each play costs one Ammo, and one Ammo is one cent. When you run out, you top up to keep going.

There is one rule worth knowing up front. Your daily free plays start building a position only once you have topped up with Ammo at least once. Before your first top-up, the five free plays are simply listening. After it, every play you make, free or paid, counts.

Building a position

When a play counts, it builds your node on that artist. The size of your node is your node power. The more node power you hold across the artists you back, the bigger your share when a drop lands.

Here is the exact amount a single play earns:

node power from a play = 1,000,000 ÷ the artist's play count so far

The "play count so far" is how many times that artist has been played, by everyone, before your play. While an artist has fewer than 100 plays, that number is held at 100, so the early plays all earn the maximum.

What that works out to:

The artist's play count so farYour play earns
The very first play10,000
The 100th play10,000
The 500th play2,000
The 1,000th play1,000
The 10,000th play100

So the first hundred plays on an artist each earn ten thousand power. From there it tapers. The thousandth play earns a thousand. The ten-thousandth earns a hundred.

Why being early matters

Read that table again, because it is the whole idea. The earliest plays on an artist are worth a hundred times what a late play is worth. If you find an artist while they are still quiet and back them before the crowd arrives, your position ends up far larger than the position of someone who shows up once that artist is already loud. The math is built to reward being right early.

Staying matters more

Backing an artist early builds a large position. Staying active is what keeps it from fading. Node power fades if you stop playing an artist:

For 24 hours after your last play, your node holds its full value. Over the next 24 hours, it fades down to zero. A single play, any time, resets the clock and stops the fade.

The power a fading node loses is not thrown away. It moves to the people still active on that same artist. So when someone drifts off an artist, the people who kept showing up absorb what the leaver gave up. Over time the board favours the people who keep showing up.

Embers

Alongside node power you carry Embers, a loyalty score. You earn one Ember for every play that counts, they never expire, and they cannot be bought. Your Ember total puts you in a tier, and your tier multiplies your share at every drop.

Node power is how much you back. Embers are how long you have shown up. A drop rewards both.

The drop

As the community plays, a momentum bar fills. When the season reaches its peak, we release a drop: a reward pool, put up by us and our partners. The pool is split across everyone holding a position, by node power and loyalty together:

your weight = your node power × your Embers multiplier your share = the pool × ( your weight ÷ everyone's weight combined )

A quick example. Say a drop pays out a pool of $1,050. Two people are holding:

HolderNode powerTierWeightShare
You40,000Backer 1.10×44,000$721.88
Someone else20,000Scout 1.00×20,000$328.13

Your share is the pool times your weight divided by the combined weight: 1,050 × 44,000 ÷ 64,000, which is $721.88. The bigger position and the higher loyalty tier both pulled your share up.

A drop does not reset anything. Your position carries straight into the next one. The only things that change it are new plays, which grow it, and going quiet, which fades it.

Cashing out

Whatever you earn from a drop lands in your balance on the You page. You cash it out in USDC on the Solana network, sent to your own wallet, whenever you choose.

What this is

The Floor rewards taking part. The pools are put up by APESONUS and its partners, so what you receive does not come from other people's money. Your Ammo pays for plays and builds a position. That position is a record of your activity, it is not an investment, and it gives you no ownership of anything. You need to be 18 or older to take part.

The music is what matters most. The Floor is a way to reward the people who keep showing up for it.
