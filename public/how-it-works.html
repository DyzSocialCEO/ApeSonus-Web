You have degeneracy syndrome and you're here for treatment. Fine. Four things move inside APESONUS: Spins, Embers, The Call, and The Draw. Here's all of it written down, because the one promise we make is receipts.

Sessions

A session is a play. Two free sessions a day, every day. Enough to taste the medicine, not enough to squat in the waiting room. When the free ones run out, Spins keep the music going.

Spins

$1 gets you 50 sessions. Your old therapist charged $200 an hour and never once understood the phrase "I sold the bottom." Spins play the music and enter The Call, and that's everything they do. They're a play credit with no cash value, they can't be sold or transferred, and nothing in the app converts them into money. We also cap how fast you can burn them, on purpose. Therapy all day every day is not recovery, it's a new addiction, and we're not running that clinic.

Embers

Every session you pay for mints one Ember. They never reset, they can't be bought, and the games never mint them. Someone who's been in treatment for a year holds more Embers than someone who walked in last week, and that's the entire point.

What do Embers do? We'll tell you when we decide. For now they're proof you were here. In this market, proof you were here is more than most people have.

The Call

Every day, one chart. Call tomorrow's five most played tracks in exact order, before the day begins. Entry is 100 Spins, about two dollars, the going rate for being sure of yourself. The counts stay sealed until the day settles, so there is nothing to copy and no scoreboard to chase. Your read of the room is the entire instrument.

The pot is real money, a published share of every dollar that came in that day, so it grows with the crowd instead of sitting at some fixed number. Call all five dead in order and you take the top of it. Miss by a little and you still land a rung, all five in any order, or four, or three, or just the number one, each rung a smaller cut than the one above. Everyone who lands the same rung splits it even, no matter what they spent to enter. Nobody calls it perfectly and the top rung rolls into tomorrow, climbing.

The Draw

Every Spin you play is also a ticket. Each night five names get pulled, weighted by tickets, and paid in USDC. First name out takes the biggest pile. One name per night, no doubles, so the whale can't eat all five chairs.

The pick is seeded from a chain blockhash taken after the day closes, which means nobody, including us, knew it while you were playing, and anyone can re-run the draw and get the same five names. Play more, hold more tickets, then put the winnings back in or take them out to your wallet. We know exactly what we built.

Getting paid

Winnings sit in your account until you want them. Once you're past five dollars, put in your Solana address and ask for it. It goes out as USDC from the house wallet and the transaction gets recorded, so you can check it landed without taking our word for anything. Check the address twice. A wrong one can't be undone.

The small print

The Call is a contest of skill, decided by your read. The Draw is chance, and it pays in the open every night. A published share of the day's money goes back to the room as prizes and the house keeps the rest to pay for the lights and the music. Spins are play credits with no cash value, and the music is the product. Everything else is a way of paying back the degens who keep showing up for it.
