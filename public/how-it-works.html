How it works

The whole system,
nothing hidden.

Three things move inside APESONUS: Spins, Embers, and reward drops. Each one does exactly one job. Here's all of it, written down so nothing about the app is a mystery.

Spins

Spins are the fuel. They play the music and they enter the games, and that's everything they do. You can pick some up in the app, or win more inside Call and Back. Spins are a play credit. They have no cash value and can't be sold or transferred — nothing in the app converts them into money.

Embers

Embers are your standing. Every track you play earns an Ember. They only come from listening — the games never mint them — and your total never resets. Someone who has been playing the catalog for a year holds more Embers than someone who arrived last week, and that's the entire point. Embers can't be bought. A daily play streak makes them come faster — hold it for seven days and every play earns triple — but listening is the only way in.

Reward drops

Every so often a partner brings a reward to the community — a drop of their token, shared out across everyone holding Embers. Your slice is pure proportion: your Embers over everyone's Embers, times the drop — hold one percent of all Embers and one percent of the drop is yours. Standing names like Believer or Diamond mark where you sit on the leaderboard; they don't change your share. Listening more is the only thing that does.

Partners fund every drop — nothing comes out of anyone's Spins, and nobody's losses pay for anybody's gains.

Call — the Conviction game

A fresh board of newly launched coins appears every day. Your job is to read it. When you're sure about one, you call it: stake your entry Spins on that coin, and the moment you do, it's claimed — your name goes on it and it leaves the board. One coin, one scout. Nobody rides your read.

Every coin carries a target and a floor, shown before you commit: the market cap it needs to reach, and the level it has to hold, inside the window on the card. Hit both and your entry comes back multiplied — 3× on a standard call, up to 5× on the hardest boards. Miss either and the Spins are spent.

Conviction is a race and a read at the same time — being right matters, and being first is what gets you the coin.

Back — the weekly pool

Once a week, every archetype on the roster competes on streams. You back the character you think finishes on top, staking at least 100 Spins, and the running numbers stay hidden until the week closes — you're backing your read of the culture, not a scoreboard.

Every stake, winning and losing, goes into one pool. When the week settles, the people who backed the right character split the entire pool. Your share is weighted by how much you staked and how early you locked it in — an early, confident back earns more of the pool than a late one of the same size. The house takes nothing out — every Spin staked goes back out to the winners.

What this is

Call and Back are contests of skill. Both are decided by your read, never by a random draw. Spins hold no cash value anywhere in the system, and neither game pays money. Embers and reward drops require no purchase at all — playing the music is all it takes, and your share arrives when a partner drop lands.

You need to be 18 or older to take part. The music is the product. Everything else is a way of rewarding the people who keep showing up for it.
